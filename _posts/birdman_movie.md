# Introduction

You can't really describe Birdman as "overrated". 

I remember during the 2015 Oscars, Birdman won four oscars (best picture, director, original screenplay, and cinematography). 

which was really exciting because I really liked the movie! 

I also remember the instant wave of backlash, where a lot of my friends *hated* the movie. I agree, there are parts of the story that are slow and not incredibly enjoyable.  


I think "Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)" (birdman) is not an overrated movie because to the amateur movie go-er it shows them how many "cuts" more popular movies use.

This is will not be an analysis of the story-line or anything, but just a focus on a single aspect of the framing in movies. 

I think a common critique I hear is that Birdman "Tries to hard", and while I can't disagree with that - I think birdman is an excellent film because it opens up the eyes to amateur movie lovers.

Birdman makes highlights the imperceivable - the things where if everything has gone right, you won't even notice. 

There are definite reasons for cutting.

# I feel like it's so difficult to see people "talking in the same screen"
I can understand why we cut between two characters talking on the screen - it's natural. We want the camera to be focused on the character talking, not on two characters conversing.
