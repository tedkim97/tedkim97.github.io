As a disclaimer - I'm going to note that I do think that there are a lot of flaws with Agile metrics, sprint burn downs, the incentives and conflict they cause, etc. I will note that I can't think of any alternatives, and burning everything to the ground would result in more problems than solutions.


When it comes to metrics measuring abstract concepts ("usefullness", "new searches", "engagement") it's important to note that metrics aren't always there for accuracy but to just be *some* number that they can use to estimate improvements/failures -- or to have *some* number to show to a business unit to justify the team's work/efforts.
